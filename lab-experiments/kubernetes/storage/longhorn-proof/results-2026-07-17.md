# Longhorn durability results, 2026-07-17

## Installed contract

- Longhorn chart and V1 data engine: `1.12.0`
- StorageClass: `longhorn`, non-default, two replicas
- Eligible workers: `talos-lqv-w4u` and `talos-opt-7040`
- Data path: `/var/mnt/longhorn`
- Longhorn node checks: required packages, mount propagation, disk readiness, and disk schedulability all `True` on both workers
- Existing default StorageClass: `local-path`

The proof claim provisioned as `pvc-5d74ea8d-dab7-4099-9934-0a4a4a544479`. Longhorn placed one running replica on each worker. The sentinel checksum was:

```text
53ddbcbecccdf06fed67a9fc5490a62a52e82031ebdb34691a2aef24bc064af3
```

## Pod recreation

The proof Deployment was recreated to add a restricted pod security context. It reattached on `talos-lqv-w4u` and returned the same checksum with both replicas running.

## Normal reboot

`talos-lqv-w4u` was cordoned and rebooted through the normal Talos reboot sequence with the proof volume attached. Talos completed the reboot command in 71 seconds. The pod reattached after the node was uncordoned, returned the same checksum, and the volume returned to `attached/healthy` with both replicas running.

The safety cordon delayed scheduling until it was removed. That delay was scheduler policy, not a storage recovery failure.

## Forced worker failure and failover

Talos does not allow its `cri` service to be stopped through the service API, so the failure test used `talosctl reboot --mode=force --wait=false`. This skips graceful teardown and is the supported reversible approximation of a worker runtime failure.

The proof Deployment was moved to `talos-opt-7040` while `talos-lqv-w4u` was unavailable. The stale pod was force-deleted. Kubernetes initially blocked the second attachment with its expected Multi-Attach guard while the old CSI attachment still existed. The external attacher cleared the stale record without manual finalizer edits, and the replacement pod became Ready on `talos-opt-7040` 126 seconds after failover was requested.

The replacement pod returned the same checksum while the volume was `attached/degraded` with the OptiPlex replica running and the Nitro replica stopped. After the Nitro worker recovered and was uncordoned, Longhorn returned the volume to `attached/healthy` with both replicas running in another 66 seconds. The checksum remained unchanged.

This proves a forced worker-runtime restart and cross-node reattachment. It does not claim a sustained physical power-loss or network-partition test.
